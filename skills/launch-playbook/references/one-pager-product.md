*One or two plain sentences. What ships, in language a smart colleague outside the team would understand. No jargon, no hype.*

### Why it matters

*The stakes. What problem this solves, who felt that pain, and why now. This is the section that earns attention — be specific about the change, not the feature.*

- **The problem before:** [What was broken, slow, or impossible.]
- **What changes now:** [The concrete shift this release creates.]

### Who it's for

*Primary audience and the one job they're trying to do. Name the segment, not "everyone."*

> [e.g. "Backend and full-stack teams maintaining services across more than two languages."]

### Key messages (the beats)

*The 3–4 things every post, email, and deck should hit. Order by importance. Pair each claim with proof.*

1. **[Headline message]** — [supporting proof / metric / detail]
2. **[Differentiator]** — [why it's credible]
3. **[Traction or scale]** — [number, customer, or milestone]
4. **[Vision / where this goes]** — [the bigger picture this unlocks]

### Positioning in one line

*The sentence you'd want repeated back to you. The tagline or "X for Y" framing.*

> ["Call functions, not endpoints."]

### Proof points

*Hard evidence anyone can cite: metrics, customer names, benchmarks, quotes. No claim above should lack a backer here.*

- [Metric / benchmark]
