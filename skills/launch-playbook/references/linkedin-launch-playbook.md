So you're announcing your fundraise or a hot new release! Congrats — this is a moment to gain visibility, leverage your networks, and plant the flag on the internet. In today's media, you as the founder are the influencer, so step it up.

**Current state of media:** It's incredibly hard to get media coverage with a seed raise and even a Series A today. Doesn't mean don't go after it, just know it's unlikely. There is a rising community of writers going independent and there are opportunities there.

## How to prep for a founder-led announcement

**Things you should do at minimum**
- LinkedIn (and X)
- Draft a blog post for your company blog

**Things you could do**
- Media outreach
- Press release (not for reach, for internet preservation)
- Product Hunt launch (it takes work but does drive traffic even if not in top 10)

## The MVP: LinkedIn

**Why?** Your network is your biggest fan. Show momentum, get your investors to amplify you, establish your profile as someone to follow.

### How to structure the LinkedIn post (founder, not company account)

*There is no point in doing this if it's not founder-led.*

**1. Hook (first 2 lines)** — make this scroll-stopping. Lead with something raw, specific, or deeply personal:
- "I started hacking on this in 2022 because nothing else worked for our stack."
- "We just raised $4.2M to make debugging distributed systems suck less."
- "Today marks a new chapter for [company name]."

**2. The Why** — ground the reader in your technical mission:
- What's broken in your space
- What you've experienced firsthand as a dev or builder
- What you're going to fix — and why it's hard
- *Example: Debugging cross-service failures shouldn't take 3 days and 6 engineers. We're building the toolkit we wish we had.*

**3. The What (funding news / product release)** — be concise and specific:
- We've raised [$X] in Seed funding led by [Firm], with participation from [names].
- We're using it to [hire backend engineers, scale infra, run more pilots].
- If hiring, include a direct CTA: *We're hiring engineers who care deeply about developer experience → [link]*

**4. The Gratitude** — give credit (tag everyone!):
- Your co-founders and early team
- First users who trusted you
- Investors who provided more than just capital
- *Example: Huge thanks to [investor], who backed us when this was just CLI duct-taped to a Notion doc.*

**5. The Ask** — be direct:
- Hiring? Link to roles — the LinkedIn algo LOVES job posts
- Looking for beta users or design partners? Say who and what kind.
- Want intros? To whom, and why?

**6. The photo** — founder photo, always.

## How to post

**A few days before**
- Email your investors/partners the launch plan (see sample email below), your goal for the announcement, and ask them to be ready to re-share.

**Day of**
- Post between 9–11am local time
- Tag the **funds** and the **investors** in the post
- Post from your personal account
- No links in the post — add the link in the first comment after you post
- Have the team reshare, comment, and like
- Reshare from your company account
- Ping your investor thread to let them know it's live, include the live link
- DM the link to friends and champions — ask for engagement

**Day after**
- Post a follow-up from your company or personal account with a product teaser, demo clip, or behind-the-scenes photo
- Reply to every comment

### Blog

Not really to drive traffic but to have an established, discoverable asset online with your news. Tell a story about:
- What technical problem you're solving
- Why it matters now (context, timing, inflection point)
- Why you and your team are uniquely qualified
- What the next technical milestone looks like with this funding

### Example LinkedIn post template

> Two years ago, I started building [product] to fix [specific technical pain point] I kept running into.
> Today, I'm excited to share that we've raised [$X] in Seed funding to keep going.
> Our mission is to [mission in one line].
> Huge thanks to [lead investor] and to [team, early users, cofounders].
> We're hiring engineers who love [insert deep technical domain] → [link in comment]
> If you're [ideal user], we'd love to hear from you — we're looking for early partners to shape the future.
